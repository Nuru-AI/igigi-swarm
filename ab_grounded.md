### 1. NVIDIA (NVDA) — STRONG BUY
**Current Price: $210.69 | RACS: 7.79 / 10**

**12-Month Price Target: $270–$290 (28–38% upside)**

#### Investment Thesis

NVIDIA occupies every critical chokepoint in the AI compute stack simultaneously: it holds the largest TSMC CoWoS-L allocation (Blackwell/GB200/Rubin architectures booked 52–78 weeks out), bilateral locked-in HBM3e supply agreements with both SK Hynix (~50% HBM market share) and Micron, and the dominant software moat through CUDA, NIM microservices, and AI Enterprise subscriptions. This is supply-chain lock-in, not merely brand strength.

Three converging forces drive margin expansion over the next 12–18 months:
- **Product mix enrichment**: GB200 NVL72 rack-scale systems bundle compute, networking, and software at higher ASPs with improving yields
- **Software attach rate**: CUDA/NIM/AI Enterprise subscriptions layer high-margin recurring revenue atop hardware — structural margin lift not fully captured in trailing P/E of ~38–42x
- **Packaging cost normalization**: CoWoS capacity scaling from 35K to 130K wafers/month compresses per-unit costs even as ASPs remain elevated

The re-entry of the H20 into China (partial relaxation of export controls) is a near-term revenue tailwind of 10–15% of revenue, though policy remains volatile.

### 2. AMD (AMD) — HOLD / SELECTIVE ACCUMULATE
**Current Price: $537.37 | RACS: 5.41 / 10**

**12-Month Price Target: $580–$640 (8–19% upside, base case ~$600)**

#### Investment Thesis

AMD is a genuine challenger in AI accelerators (MI300X/MI325X), and hyperscaler interest in AMD as a cost/performance alternative to NVIDIA is real. However, the bull case is supply-gated, not demand-gated: AMD's TSMC CoWoS-S allocation is materially smaller than NVIDIA's and insufficient to support a step-change in market share within 12 months. The forward EPS ramp to $12–$15 (2026E, implying a forward P/E of ~35–45x on current trailing P/E of 175–210x) requires flawless execution on a path that runs directly through the CoWoS and HBM3e bottlenecks that currently favor NVIDIA.

AMD's margin trajectory is improving but supply-gated. The pricing power AMD does have is bounded — it cannot price aggressively above market without reliable volume delivery, and that volume delivery depends on TSMC allocation decisions that currently prioritize NVIDIA and hyperscaler custom ASICs (Google TPU, Amazon Trainium).

The Hold/Selective Accumulate rating reflects: (1) genuine long-term optionality if supply allocation shifts materially, (2) unacceptable valuation risk at current multiples if execution slips even one quarter, and (3) asymmetric downside in a rate-rise or macro shock scenario given AMD's extreme duration sensitivity (a 100bps rate rise compresses fair value 15–22%).
