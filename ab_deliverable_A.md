## 3. Semiconductor Supply-Chain Signals

The supply-chain picture is the single most important structural driver for this sector:

### CoWoS Advanced Packaging (TSMC)
- **The binding bottleneck:** CoWoS (Chip-on-Wafer-on-Substrate) — which co-packages HBM with AI accelerators — is sold out through 2025 and well into 2026.
- TSMC CoWoS capacity is estimated at ~75,000 wafers/month in 2025, ramping toward 120,000–130,000 wafers/month by end of 2026. Even at max ramp, four leading AI chip designers consumed ~90% of global CoWoS capacity in 2025.
- This is a structural constraint, not a transient one. Competitors cannot easily replicate TSMC's CoWoS yield or scale.

### HBM (High Bandwidth Memory)
- SK Hynix CFO stated: **"We have already sold out our entire 2026 HBM supply."**
- Micron CEO confirmed: **"Our HBM capacity for calendar 2025 and 2026 is fully booked."**
- Traditional DRAM prices rose up to **50% in late 2025** due to wafer and packaging availability constraints from HBM demand diversion.
- Technical focus is shifting from stack height to bandwidth engineering — RDL (Redistribution Layer) and PDN (Power Delivery Network) design are emerging as the next performance bottleneck.

### Investment Implication of Supply Chain:
- NVDA benefits the most as the dominant consumer of CoWoS + HBM (H100/H200/B200 series), allowing it to maintain gross margins above 70% and extract pricing power.
- AMD benefits secondarily through MI300X/MI400 ramp; gaining CoWoS allocation is its critical execution hurdle.
- Supply constraints make the semi-equipment and advanced packaging sub-sector (ASML, Amkor, ASE) attractive as second-order beneficiaries, though outside this memo's primary scope.

## 4. Risk Factors

1. **Valuation Compression Risk:** Both NVDA and AMD trade at significant earnings multiples. Any guidance miss or macro deterioration could trigger violent de-ratings.
2. **Geopolitical / Export Controls:** U.S. export restrictions on advanced AI chips to China remain a wildcard that could remove a meaningful revenue pool.
3. **CoWoS Capacity Relief:** If TSMC aggressively expands packaging capacity beyond 130K wafers/month by end-2026, pricing power erodes.
4. **Customer Concentration:** Hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, Meta, Amazon) represent a large majority of NVDA revenue; any capex pullback reverberates immediately.
5. **Crypto-AI Tail Risk:** Broader crypto risk-off or regulatory action could compress AI token valuations independent of fundamental progress.
6. **AMD Execution Risk:** MI300X/MI400 ramp success depends on software ecosystem (ROCm maturity) catching up to CUDA — a persistent competitive disadvantage.
