### 1. NVIDIA (NVDA) — BUY | Highest Conviction

**52-Week Range: ~$86–$211 | Trailing P/E: ~45–55x**

**Recommendation: BUY with a 12-month base-case price target of $155–$185 on modest deceleration, and a bull-case target of $240–$270 on continued Blackwell/Rubin execution.**

NVDA represents the highest-conviction long in the AI-compute universe. The stock is breaking out to new 52-week highs on extraordinary institutional volume (241M+ shares on a +2.95% day), signaling professional accumulation, not retail speculation. The investment thesis is supply-chain-validated: TSMC CoWoS advanced packaging is sold out through 2026, with NVIDIA consuming over half of all CoWoS output. This physical constraint — not narrative — is the most credible third-party confirmation of real hyperscaler demand from AWS, Azure, Google Cloud, and Oracle. The $700B+ in Big Tech AI capex committed for 2026 alone underpins multi-quarter revenue visibility.

**Bull thesis drivers:** Blackwell GPU commercial dominance; multi-year CoWoS and HBM3e allocation lock; CUDA software ecosystem moat compounds hardware lead; 62 analyst Strong Buy ratings; inference demand scaling for agentic AI workflows; export restrictions paradoxically accelerating front-loading in unrestricted markets (US, EU, Japan, Middle East).

**Three-scenario framework:**
- **Base Case (60% probability):** Revenue growth decelerates to 35–40% in FY2027; P/E compresses to 35–40x; **price target $155–$185** (downside of -12% to -26% from $210.69).
- **Bull Case (25% probability):** Blackwell HBM3e yields beat; Rubin architecture pre-orders accelerate; P/E sustains at 50x on higher EPS; **price target $240–$270** (upside of +14% to +28%).
- **Bear Case (15% probability):** HBM3e yield crisis at SK Hynix delays Blackwell ramp 1–2 quarters; macro risk-off causes hyperscaler capex pause; P/E compresses to 25x; **price target $95–$120** (downside of -43% to -55%).

**Strategic view:** At current prices, NVDA offers asymmetric upside given order-book visibility. The P/E of 45–55x is elevated versus the S&P 500 (~22x) but is defensible given the 50–60% data-center revenue CAGR. We treat the base case as a healthy consolidation within a durable bull trend. Position sizing should reflect the 15% tail-risk bear scenario. Recommended portfolio weight: **overweight, 5–8% of a diversified growth portfolio**, with a stop-loss discipline at $155 (base-case low end).

### 2. AMD (Advanced Micro Devices) — BUY | Moderate Conviction (Rotation Play)
**Current Price: ~$108–$115 | Trailing P/E: ~150–180x**
**52-Week Range: ~$71–$187 | Currently ~39–42% below 52-week high**

**Recommendation: BUY for momentum/rotation exposure with a base-case price target of $120–$145, and a bull-case target of $165–$190.**

AMD is the most compelling near-term rotation trade in the AI-compute sector. The stock has dramatically underperformed NVDA's breakout to new highs (AMD remains ~40% below its 52-week high of $187), yet the fundamental re-rating catalyst is in progress: Goldman Sachs and Bernstein both upgraded AMD to Buy in May 2026, citing the agentic AI CPU wave. CEO Lisa Su's confirmation — *"Agents are really driving tremendous demand in the overall AI adoption cycle"* — positions AMD's CPU-server franchise as a structural beneficiary of inference scaling at the edge of the AI stack.

**Bull thesis drivers:** CPU tailwinds from agentic AI workloads; MI300X GPU ramp providing NVDA competitive alternative for cost-sensitive hyperscalers; analyst re-rating momentum still in early innings; valuation below 52-week highs offers better entry than NVDA; earnings re-rating potential if MI300X volumes scale.

**Three-scenario framework:**
- **Base Case (55% probability):** MI300X ramp continues; CPU agentic AI thesis validates; earnings grow 30–40%; P/E compresses toward 80–100x on higher EPS; **price target $120–$145** (upside of +8% to +31% from ~$111 midpoint).
- **Bull Case (25% probability):** MI400 ramp accelerates; agentic AI CPU surge drives EPS re-rating; P/E expands on earnings beat; **price target $165–$190** (upside of +49% to +71%).
- **Bear Case (20% probability):** CoWoS allocation worsens; hyperscalers double down on custom ASICs (Broadcom/Marvell); P/E compresses to 50–60x on flat earnings; **price target $55–$75** (downside of -32% to -50%).

**Key risk:** AMD's 150–180x trailing P/E is the most dangerous valuation in this coverage universe. The multiple is only defensible if the earnings re-rating arrives within 2–3 quarters. Secondary CoWoS allocation (behind NVDA) is a structural supply constraint that caps near-term acceleration. The bear case probability at 20% is meaningfully higher than NVDA's 15%, reflecting execution risk.

**Strategic view:** AMD offers a better risk/reward entry point than NVDA for investors who missed the NVDA breakout. The relative underperformance is an opportunity, not a red flag, given the analyst upgrade cycle. Recommended portfolio weight: **moderate overweight, 3–5% of a diversified growth portfolio**, sized smaller than NVDA to reflect higher execution risk and a more elevated bear case probability.
